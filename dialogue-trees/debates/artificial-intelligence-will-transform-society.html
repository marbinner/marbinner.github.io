Artificial intelligence will transform society

Background & Definitions

Background

The recent rapid advancements in AI, particularly large language models and generative capabilities, have intensified predictions of immense societal change. This debate centers on whether the resulting changes represent a fundamental "transformation" that permanently redefines human institutions, like labor, governance, and knowledge, or if they are merely a significant, albeit manageable, technological evolution akin to the internet or industrial revolution.

Key Definitions

Transformation
A fundamental and irreversible alteration to the structure of human institutions, relationships, or cultural norms.
Artificial Intelligence (AI)
Systems capable of exhibiting behaviors characteristic of intelligence, such as learning, reasoning, and complex problem-solving, operating significantly beyond pre-programmed automation.
General-Purpose AI Systems (GPAIS)
AI technologies, such as large language models, designed to perform a wide variety of tasks across multiple domains rather than specialized narrow functions.
