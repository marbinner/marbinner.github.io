Free will is an illusion

Background & Definitions

Background

The question of whether humans possess genuine free will is a central and ancient philosophical debate, recently amplified by advancements in neuroscience and physics. Deterministic models of the universe suggest that all events, including human decisions, are pre-determined by antecedent causes, challenging the notion of conscious, uncaused choice. This issue has profound societal implications for moral responsibility, the justice system, and the fundamental concept of human agency.

Key Definitions

Free will
A contested concept regarding the ability of agents to make choices. Libertarians define it as uncaused choice independent of prior causes; Compatibilists define it as the freedom to act according to one's motives, even if those motives are ultimately determined; Hard Determinists argue it does not exist.
