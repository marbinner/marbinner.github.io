Universal basic income would reduce poverty

Background & Definitions

Background

Universal Basic Income (UBI) is a widely debated policy proposal intended to provide an unconditional financial safety net and address rising economic inequality and job displacement due to automation. The debate centers on whether the economic benefits of poverty reduction outweigh the feasibility challenges, such as the immense fiscal cost, potential inflationary pressures, and the risk of labor market disincentives. Different interpretations of what constitutes UBI and how poverty is measured often lead to semantic disagreements that distract from substantive policy analysis.

Key Definitions

Universal Basic Income (UBI)
A periodic cash payment unconditionally delivered to all individual residents, without an income, wealth, or work requirement.
Poverty
A state where individuals lack the financial resources to meet a designated minimum standard of living, frequently measured as a lack of income below a predetermined threshold.
Reduce
To substantially decrease the number of people falling below the defined income poverty threshold or to lessen the average depth of the income deficit among the poor.
