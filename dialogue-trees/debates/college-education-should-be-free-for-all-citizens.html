College education should be free for all citizens

Background & Definitions

Background

The proposition centers on the rising costs of higher education and its impact on debt and social mobility. Proponents view post-secondary education as a necessary public good that should be universally accessible for a competitive economy. Opponents argue against the massive budgetary expenditures required, question the necessity of public subsidy for private career benefits, and debate the scope of what constitutes 'free' and 'college education.'

Key Definitions

Free
The waiver of all associated costs necessary for attendance, including tuition, mandatory fees, books, and basic living stipends.
College education
Any accredited post-secondary non-degree vocational training or degree-granting program up to and including the level of a bachelor's degree.
Citizens
All permanent legal residents and naturalized citizens of the country sponsoring the policy.
