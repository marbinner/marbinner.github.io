Artificial intelligence poses an existential risk to humanity

Background & Definitions

Background

The rapid advancement of deep learning and sophisticated large language models has renewed concerns about the eventual development of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) that could surpass human intellectual capabilities. The core disagreement centers on whether such hypothetical superior intelligence could be fundamentally misaligned with human values, leading to catastrophic or irreversible harm, or if the current focus on existential risk is premature and distracts from addressing the immediate, tangible harms of current narrow AI technologies.

Key Definitions

Artificial Intelligence (AI)
The term encompasses current Narrow AI systems that perform specific tasks, and hypothetical advanced systems like Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) or Artificial Superintelligence (ASI), which could match or exceed human cognitive abilities across all domains.
Existential Risk
A risk that threatens the destruction of humanity‚Äôs long-term potential, leading to human extinction or an unrecoverable collapse of civilization.
