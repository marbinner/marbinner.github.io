Physicalism is the best metaphysical theory

Background & Definitions

Background

Physicalism is the dominant metaphysical position in contemporary analytic philosophy, maintaining that everything that exists is fundamentally physical or supervenes on the physical. The proposition questions whether physicalism is superior to competing theories, such as substance dualism, property dualism, or idealism, which posit non-physical substances or mental primacy. Evaluating the "best" theory often revolves around its explanatory power regarding consciousness, its coherence, and its compatibility with the findings of modern science.

Key Definitions

Physicalism
A contested theory maintaining that everything that exists is fundamentally physical, or that all facts about the world are ultimately determined by physical facts. Versions range from reductive physicalism, which asserts that mental states are strictly identical to physical states, to non-reductive physicalism, which posits that mental properties supervene on physical states but are not wholly reducible to them.
