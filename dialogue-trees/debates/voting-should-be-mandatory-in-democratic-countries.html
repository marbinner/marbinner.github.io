Voting should be mandatory in democratic countries

Background & Definitions

Background

The debate over mandatory voting pits the goal of maximizing political legitimacy and universal citizen engagement against the fundamental right to individual choice and freedom of expression (including the right not to vote). Proponents argue it reduces socioeconomic bias in the electorate and increases the representativeness of political outcomes. Critics, however, view it as an oppressive government overreach that forces uninformed citizens to the polls, potentially reducing the quality of deliberation.

Key Definitions

Mandatory Voting
A legal requirement that citizens attend a polling place or return a ballot, often with the specific option allowed to cast a blank or spoiled vote without penalty.
Civic Duty
The moral or legal obligation felt or imposed upon citizens to contribute to the well-being and effective functioning of their society, distinct from personal interests.
Politics Voting Democracy
Proposition: Voting should be mandatory in democratic countries
