The mind is identical to the brain

Background & Definitions

Background

The proposition addresses the foundational mind-body problem in philosophy, centered on the relationship between mental properties and physical properties. While contemporary neuroscience strongly correlates mental states with specific brain activity, the nature of subjective, first-person experience (qualia) remains metaphysically difficult to reconcile entirely with purely physical matter. Proponents argue for ontological reduction (identity theory), while opponents maintain that the mind is either an emergent, non-reducible property or a distinct substance (dualism).

Key Definitions

Consciousness/Mind
Consciousness (or mind) is the state of subjective awareness and experience. Physicalists (including identity theorists) argue it is fully reducible to and explainable by physical brain states. Dualists and non-reductive physicalists argue that subjective features (qualia) are an irreducible property or phenomenon distinct from physical matter.
