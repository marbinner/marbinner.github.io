Social media harms mental health

Background & Definitions

Background

The rapid and near-universal adoption of social media platforms has raised significant public and scientific concern, coinciding with documented increases in mental health challenges, notably among youth. While correlation studies suggest a link between increased usage and declining well-being (e.g., higher rates of depression and anxiety), the causality remains disputed, with critics arguing that social media offers substantial benefits in connection, community, and information access. The debate concerns the nature, magnitude, and specific mechanisms of any measured negative effects versus potential positive outcomes.

Key Definitions

Social Media
Internet-based platforms that enable users to create and share content, participate in social networking, and interact primarily through mobile or desktop applications, such as Instagram, TikTok, and X.
Mental Health
A state of psychological, emotional, and social well-being that relates both to the absence of diagnosed mental disorders (e.g., anxiety, depression, disordered eating) and subjective measures of behavioral function and life satisfaction.
Harms
Negative consequences or deleterious effects arising from the use of social media, ranging from mild distress and social comparison to clinically significant psychological impairment.
