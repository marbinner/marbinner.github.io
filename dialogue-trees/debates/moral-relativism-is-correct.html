Moral relativism is correct

Background & Definitions

Background

The debate surrounding moral relativism centers on whether morality is universal and objective or contingent upon cultural, historical, or individual contexts. Proponents argue that the vast diversity in moral practices across the globe demonstrates that no single, overarching moral truth exists. Opponents maintain that discarding objective morality undermines the possibility of meaningful moral criticism, universal human rights, and the foundational structure of justice.

Key Definitions

Moral Relativism
Moral Relativism is a family of meta-ethical theories asserting that the truth or falsity of moral judgments is contingent upon the standards of a group (cultural relativism) or an individual (moral subjectivism), contrasting with the view that moral claims are true or false independently of human opinions.
