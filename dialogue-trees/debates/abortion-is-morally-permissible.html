Abortion is morally permissible

Background

Background

The debate over the moral permissibility of abortion hinges on an irreconcilable conflict between competing fundamental ethical principles: the bodily autonomy and self-determination of the pregnant person versus the moral status and potential right to life of the developing fetus. Societal and legal disagreement primarily centers on when the biological entity acquires "personhood," and whether a potential right to life can override the established right to control one‚Äôs own body and life decisions.
