Social media does more harm than good

Background & Definitions

Background

Social media platforms have become an integral part of modern life, radically altering communication, commerce, and political organization globally. This proposition forces a difficult cost-benefit analysis, requiring a comparison between the massive positive impacts (e.g., connectivity, economic opportunities) and equally significant negative consequences (e.g., mental health crises, political polarization, proliferation of misinformation). The debate hinges on which set of effects has a greater aggregate magnitude across society.

Key Definitions

Social Media
Internet-based platforms that enable users to create and share content or to participate in social networking, including services like X, Facebook, and TikTok.
Harm
Negative societal or individual consequences, specifically including losses of privacy, damage to mental health, and the erosion of common factual bases due to misinformation.
Good
Positive societal or individual consequences, specifically including increased economic opportunity, enhanced global communication, and the facilitation of social or political mobilization.
