Euthanasia should be legal

Background & Definitions

Background

The debate over legalizing euthanasia balances the principle of individual autonomy and the provision of compassionate relief from unbearable suffering against concerns over the sanctity of human life and the potential for coercion of vulnerable individuals. Opponents cite the ethical integrity of the medical profession and the potential for abuse, while proponents focus on patient dignity and freedom from intolerable pain. Jurisdictions adopt varied approaches, leading to ongoing legal and ethical disputes worldwide.

Key Definitions

Euthanasia
The intentional ending of a life, usually by a medical professional, to relieve intractable suffering; it is often distinguished from Physician-Assisted Suicide (PAS), where the doctor provides the means (e.g., medication) but the patient administers the lethal dose, and Passive Euthanasia (withholding life-sustaining treatment).
