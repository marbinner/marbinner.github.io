Idealism is the best metaphysical theory

Background & Definitions

Background

Idealism is a core metaphysical position arguing that reality is fundamentally mental or consciousness-based, in direct opposition to physicalism, which holds that reality is primarily matter-based. The long-standing debate centers on which theory best accounts for the apparent objectivity of the external world, the nature of conscious experience, and integrating the findings of modern science. Philosophers argue over which framework provides the most coherent, complete, and parsimonious explanation for existence.

Key Definitions

Metaphysical Theory
The branch of philosophy concerned with the fundamental nature of being, reality, and the world, addressing questions about existence, substance, purpose, and cosmology.
Idealism
The philosophical position that the structure of reality is fundamentally consciousness, mind, or spirit, ranging from subjective forms (reality depends on the individual mind) to objective forms (reality is a manifestation of an ultimate, universal cosmic mind).
