Climate change is primarily caused by human activity

Background & Definitions

Background

While the vast majority of scientific academies and international bodies agree that global climate change since the mid-20th century is overwhelmingly driven by human influence, the proposition remains intensely debated in political and public discourse. This disagreement centers primarily on determining the precise ratio of anthropogenic causes versus long-term natural climate variability (such as solar cycles, orbital changes, or volcanic activity). Resolving this attribution question is fundamental to determining the necessity, urgency, and scale of policy interventions.

Key Definitions

Climate Change
Long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns; scientifically, this refers to statistically significant changes in the climate system observed over decades or longer.
Human Activity
Activities directly traceable to human action, specifically the large-scale industrial processes, massive land-use changes, and the emission of greenhouse gases (GHGs) into the atmosphere.
Primarily Caused
The argument focuses on whether human actions account for the majority of observed climate forcings (more than 50%) since the start of the Industrial Revolution, establishing human influence as the dominant factor compared to natural drivers.
