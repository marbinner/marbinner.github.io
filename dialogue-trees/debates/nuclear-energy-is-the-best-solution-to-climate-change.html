Nuclear energy is the best solution to climate change

Background & Definitions

Background

Addressing climate change requires transitioning global energy systems away from fossil fuels rapidly. Nuclear energy is often promoted as a critical component due to its high capacity factor and near-zero operational carbon emissions, contrasting with intermittent renewable sources. The debate centers on whether the benefits of nuclear power, such as scalability and reliability, outweigh the significant risks and drawbacks associated with high upfront costs, lengthy deployment schedules, and the management of long-term radioactive waste.

Key Definitions

Best Solution
The optimal strategy for global decarbonization, judged by a composite metric of speed of deployment, total system cost, system reliability, safety profile, and minimal long-term waste burden.

Proposition: Nuclear energy is the best solution to climate change
