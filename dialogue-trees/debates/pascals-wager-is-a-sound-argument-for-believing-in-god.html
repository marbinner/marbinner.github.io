Pascal's Wager is a sound argument for believing in God

Background

Background

Pascal's Wager is a classic philosophical argument posited by Blaise Pascal, which contends that believing in God is the most rational decision because the potential payoff (eternal bliss) infinitely outweighs the potential cost (a finite, secular life). The debate centers on whether such a pragmatic, decision-theoretic approach based on expected utility successfully translates into a sound reason for *genuine* belief, as opposed to mere feigned action. Critics frequently challenge the argument's structure, notably via the "many Gods" or "inconsistent revelations" objection, arguing that the wager fails to specify which of the world's contradictory religious systems one should bet on.
