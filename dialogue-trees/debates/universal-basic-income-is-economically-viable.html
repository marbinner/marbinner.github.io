Universal basic income is economically viable

Background & Definitions

Background

Universal Basic Income (UBI) is widely proposed as a potential response to growing income inequality, poverty, and job displacement from automation. The primary controversy surrounding UBI focuses less on its social desirability and more on the immense macroeconomic challenge of funding its implementation. Debate centers on whether generating the necessary revenue through new taxes or budgetary restructuring is fiscally feasible without creating damaging inflationary effects or collapsing the labor market.

Key Definitions

Universal Basic Income (UBI)
An unconditional, periodic cash payment delivered to all individual residents regardless of income, wealth, or employment status.
