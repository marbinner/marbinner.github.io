Globalization has been net positive for humanity

Background & Definitions

Background

This proposition addresses the comprehensive impact of interconnected global systems, primarily stemming from accelerated trade liberalization and technological diffusion since the latter half of the 20th century. Proponents focus on macroeconomic benefits, citing historical reductions in absolute poverty and increased wealth generation. Critics emphasize negative consequences such as deepening economic inequality within nations, significant environmental damage, and the erosion of national sovereignty and cultural homogeneity.

Key Definitions

Globalization
The increasing worldwide integration of economic, political, and cultural systems, facilitated by cross-border trade, technology, and flows of finance and people.
