Vaccines cause autism

Background & Definitions

Background

The proposition originated from a now-retracted and discredited 1998 paper alleging a link between the Measles, Mumps, and Rubella (MMR) vaccine and the onset of autism. Despite overwhelming scientific consensus globally that finds no causal link, the claim persists due to historical controversy and continuous misinformation campaigns. The debate centers on reconciling robust epidemiological evidence with public anxiety regarding vaccine safety and the rise in autism diagnoses.

Key Definitions

Vaccines
Biological preparations administered to stimulate the body's immune system and provide active acquired immunity against a specific infectious disease.
Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
A neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by difficulties with social interaction and communication, and by restricted or repetitive patterns of behavior, interests, or activities.
Cause
In scientific and epidemiological discourse, a statistically significant and biologically plausible relationship where one factor produces a measurable effect that would not occur otherwise.
