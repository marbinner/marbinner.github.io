Retributive punishment is justified

Background & Definitions

Background

The justification for state-imposed punishment is foundational to criminal justice systems and divides theories into two main camps. Retributive justice argues that punishment is justified solely because the offender deserves it, acting as repayment for a moral wrong committed in the past. This contrasts sharply with consequentialist approaches, such as deterrence or rehabilitation, which justify punishment based on its beneficial future effects on society rather than past actions.

Key Definitions

Retributive Punishment
A theory of justice where the justification for punishment is based solely on the fact that an offense has been committed, holding that the severity of the sanction should be proportional to the moral gravity of the past offense.
