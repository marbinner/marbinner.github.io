Humans should colonize Mars

Background & Definitions

Background

The proposition to colonize Mars is framed as a critical debate over humanity's long-term future. Proponents view it as the ultimate firewall against civilization-ending (existential) risks on Earth, while also driving radical technological innovation. Opponents argue that the immense cost, extreme physiological challenges, and ethical burden of planetary preservation make the endeavor unjustifiable compared to investing resources in solving current crises on Earth.

Key Definitions

Colonization (of Mars)
The establishment of a permanent human population on Mars that is biologically and materially capable of long-term survival and growth without continuous reliance on Earth-based resupply.
Self-Sustaining
The ability of a Martian settlement to independently generate all necessary resources‚Äîair, water, food, power, and essential replacement parts‚Äîrequired to maintain its population without external support.
