War can be morally justified

Background

Background

The moral justification of war is a central and ancient philosophical debate, traditionally framed by the criteria of Just War theory. Critics, particularly pacifists, argue that the inherent and massive civilian suffering and destruction caused by armed conflict can never be aligned with moral principles, regardless of the cause. Proponents maintain that, as a last resort, war can be a necessary evil to prevent catastrophic human rights violations, defend national sovereignty, or resist imminent, unjust aggression.
