Social media companies should be held liable for user-generated content

Background & Definitions

Background

This proposition challenges existing legal frameworks, such as Section 230 in the U.S., which often immunize social media platforms from liability arising from content published by their users. The debate hinges on whether these companies should be treated as 'distributors' or 'publishers'‚Äîa distinction critical to holding them financially and legally accountable for harmful content like hate speech, misinformation, and copyright infringement disseminated on their sites. Critics argue that current immunity encourages corporate negligence, while supporters warn that revoking it would lead to hyper-censorship and severe restrictions on online free speech.

Key Definitions

Liability
Legal or financial responsibility for harm caused by content, obligating the liable party to pay damages or face statutory penalties.
Social media company
Online services primarily dedicated to facilitating user interaction and hosting third-party content, typically treated legally as platforms rather than original publishers.
User-Generated Content (UGC)
Any form of media, including text, videos, or images, that is publicly posted by users of the platform rather than by the company itself.
