Cryptocurrency should be regulated like traditional currencies

Background & Definitions

Background

Cryptocurrencies currently operate under inconsistent or fragmented regulatory regimes globally, leading to uncertainty regarding their classification and oversight. Proponents of regulation seek to mitigate risks to financial stability, prevent illicit use, and ensure investor protection by applying existing supervision models. Opponents argue that established financial frameworks stifle the core technological innovation of decentralization and are ultimately ill-suited for these digital assets.

Key Definitions

Cryptocurrency
A digital asset designed to work as a medium of exchange secured by cryptography and decentralized transaction ledgers, whose regulatory status as a currency, security, or commodity is contested.
Traditional Currencies
Fiat money issued and controlled by a sovereign government's central bank and typically granted status as legal tender.
Regulated
The imposition of rules governing financial market access, disclosure, capital requirements, investor protection, and anti-money laundering controls.
Economics Cryptocurrency Finance
Proposition: Cryptocurrency should be regulated like traditional currencies
