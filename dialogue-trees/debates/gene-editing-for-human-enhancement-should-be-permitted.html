Gene editing for human enhancement should be permitted

Background & Definitions

Background

Gene editing technologies, particularly CRISPR, make it feasible to alter the human genome. While therapeutic use for curing diseases is generally accepted, non-therapeutic use for "enhancement"‚Äîimproving traits like strength or intelligence‚Äîis highly controversial. The debate centers on the safety and permanence of inheritable changes, the ethical implications of fundamentally altering the human condition, and the potential for deepening socioeconomic inequality by making advanced genetics accessible only to the wealthy.

Key Definitions

Gene Editing
The targeted alteration of a DNA sequence within a living organism, typically using tools like CRISPR-Cas9.
Human Enhancement
Non-therapeutic genetic modifications (those beyond treating disease) intended to intentionally improve capacities or characteristics beyond what is considered normal or healthy for a human being.
