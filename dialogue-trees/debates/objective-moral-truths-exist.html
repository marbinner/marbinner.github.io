Objective moral truths exist

Background & Definitions

Background

This proposition addresses the foundational question of meta-ethics: whether moral claims are factual statements describing a mind-independent reality (moral realism) or merely expressions of human feeling, cultural preferences, or social commands (moral anti-realism). Proponents argue that the near-universal condemnation of certain acts, like torture or large-scale violence, implies a common objective standard discoverable by reason. Opponents point to vast historical and cultural variations in moral practices as evidence that morality is fundamentally subjective or relativistic, making any claim of universal truth unsustainable.

Key Definitions

Objective moral truths
Statements about right and wrong whose truth value is independent of human beliefs, feelings, or social conventions; this view is challenged by moral anti-realism, which variously holds that morality is subjective (based on individual feelings), relative (based on cultural norms), or non-existent (error theory).
