Censorship can be morally justified

Background & Definitions

Background

The debate hinges on the ethical tension between the fundamental right to free expression and the necessity of preventing specific societal harms, such as incitement to violence, public health misinformation, or the protection of minors. Proponents argue that consequentialist concerns justify certain limitations when the prevented harm outweighs the restriction on speech. Opponents maintain that any allowance for official censorship inherently undermines democratic principles and the necessary pursuit of truth.

Key Definitions

Censorship
The suppression of speech, public communication, or information. The term is contested regarding the agent: traditional usage refers to state bodies restricting expression, but contemporary usage often includes content moderation and removal actions taken by private platforms.
