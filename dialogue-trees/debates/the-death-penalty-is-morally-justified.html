The death penalty is morally justified

Background

Background

The use of capital punishment remains one of the most divisive ethical and legal issues globally, with a large majority of nations having abolished the practice in law or in practice, while others, including China, Iran, and some U.S. states, continue to utilize it. Proponents argue for its necessity based on proportional justice (retribution) or as a superior deterrent against violent crime, such as murder. Opponents argue that the state lacks the moral authority to intentionally take a life, citing the inherent right to life and the irreversible risk of executing innocent individuals.
