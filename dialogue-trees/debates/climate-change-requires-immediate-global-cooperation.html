Climate change requires immediate global cooperation

Background & Definitions

Background

Climate change is a scientifically verified global externality whose mitigation necessitates coordinated action across diverse political and economic systems. The debate is not whether climate action is necessary, but rather the degree of urgency and the mechanism by which it should be achieved, challenging notions of national sovereignty and equitable burden-sharing. This proposition asserts that the scale of the crisis overrides national self-interest and demands an immediate, unified international response.

Key Definitions

Requires
The proposed action is the only viable or effective path to mitigating the most severe physical, social, and economic damages resulting from climate change.
Immediate
Action beginning within the current geopolitical and investment cycle, prioritizing radical policy and behavioral shifts over gradual implementation.
Global Cooperation
A system of coordinated international actions, including binding agreements, shared financial contributions, and technology transfer, aimed at achieving universal measurable emissions targets.
