Climate change mitigation should prioritize adaptation over prevention

Background & Definitions

Background

This debate centers on the optimal strategy for deploying finite resources‚Äîfinancial, political, and technical‚Äîin response to the climate crisis. Prevention (reducing emissions) addresses the root cause but requires massive international coordination and yields long-term results, while adaptation (building resilience) addresses immediate and imminent threats and is locally actionable. The proposition requires choosing which of these two necessary strategies should receive primary focus.

Key Definitions

Prevention
Prevention refers specifically to policy and technological actions designed to curb the root cause of climate change, namely global greenhouse gas emissions.
Adaptation
Adaptation refers to adjusting ecological, social, or economic systems in response to actual or expected climatic stimuli and their effects, specifically to reduce vulnerability or enhance resilience.
Prioritize
Prioritize means strategically allocating a disproportionately larger share of available financial, political, and material resources toward one strategy over the other.

Proposition: Climate change mitigation should prioritize adaptation over prevention
