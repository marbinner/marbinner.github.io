Philosophy should be a mandatory subject in schools

Background & Definitions

Background

The proposition addresses a long-standing debate over the purpose of public education: should it prioritize vocational skills and measurable outcomes, or cultivate broad intellectual capacity and civic virtue? Proponents argue that philosophy is essential for developing critical thinking and ethical literacy, while opponents cite limited curriculum time and the need to prioritize subjects with more direct career utility, such as STEM or vocational training.

Key Definitions

Mandatory Subject
A required, independently graded, and dedicated course of study that every student must complete to graduate, occupying a regular spot in the school timetable.
Philosophy
A distinct course of study focusing on formal logic, critical reasoning, practical ethics, epistemology, and the history of core philosophical problems, separate from civics or religious instruction.
