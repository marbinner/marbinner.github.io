Consciousness can be fully explained by neuroscience

Background & Definitions

Background

The proposition addresses the "hard problem" of consciousness‚Äîhow subjective, first-person experience (qualia) arises from physical, third-person brain matter. Neuroscientists often adopt a physicalist or reductionist position, arguing that complete mapping of neural correlates will fully explain consciousness as a process. Conversely, many philosophers argue that the subjective quality is fundamentally irreducible and cannot be captured solely by studying brain anatomy or function.

Key Definitions

Consciousness
Consciousness refers to the state of being aware of one's internal and external existence; the central philosophical debate is whether this is purely a matter of information processing (access consciousness) or involves irreducible subjective experience (phenomenal consciousness, or qualia).
