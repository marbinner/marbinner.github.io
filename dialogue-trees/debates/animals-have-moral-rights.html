Animals have moral rights

Background & Definitions

Background

The proposition challenges the traditional anthropocentric view that moral rights belong exclusively to humans due to rationality or moral agency. The debate addresses whether sentience and the capacity to suffer are sufficient criteria for assigning moral rights to non-human animals. This question fundamentally impacts human practices in agriculture, scientific research, and entertainment, generating significant philosophical disagreement.

Key Definitions

Moral Rights
Moral rights is a contested concept: Rights advocates (deontologists) define them as inherent protections based on being a 'subject-of-a-life,' irrespective of utility. Utilitarians focus on minimizing suffering and maximizing well-being (welfare). Traditional systems restrict moral rights exclusively to rational or autonomous human agents.
