Quantum mechanics proves consciousness is fundamental

Background & Definitions

Background

This proposition connects the highly complex fields of quantum physics and the philosophy of mind. The debate stems from certain interpretations of quantum mechanics, specifically the 'measurement problem,' where an observer's act appears to cause the quantum wave function to collapse from a probabilistic state to a definite one. Advocates use this observation to argue that consciousness must be a fundamental, active component of reality rather than merely an emergent biological phenomenon.

Key Definitions

Consciousness
The state of subjective awareness (qualia). This is a contested concept: physicalists argue it is an emergent property of the brain and complex nervous systems, while dualists argue it is a non-physical, irreducible phenomenon.
Quantum Mechanics
Quantum Mechanics (QM) is the branch of physics describing nature at the atomic and subatomic scales. In this context, the debate relies on the interpretation of the measurement problem, where the act of observation seemingly causes the quantum wave function to collapse into a single state.
