Abortion after the third trimester is immoral

Background

Background

Abortion is one of the most polarizing ethical issues, and debates over late-term procedures are especially intense due to the high likelihood of fetal viability. The third trimester marks the point where the fetus is often viable outside the womb, leading opponents to argue the procedure constitutes the termination of a nearly fully formed human life, thus triggering stronger moral objections. Proponents focus on the rare circumstances, such as severe threats to the mother‚Äôs health or the detection of otherwise undiagnosed, debilitating fetal anomalies, which necessitate such difficult and often delayed decisions.
